Diet Information

Foxes are omnivores, meaning they eat both meat as well as plants. They like to eat smaller animals like rabbits, rodents, and birds; they also like to eat berries and different fruits. Generally foxes eat around 500 milligrams of food per day.

Mating and Reproduction Information

Gestation Period: 49-58 days How many offspring do foxes typically have at a time? Typically, 4-5 cubs at a time Foxes typically live with their parents for seven months after being born

Animal Behavior

Friendly and curious Foxes like to play with other animals Foxes are territorial animals, they create and defend their territory against other foxes Foxes use defense mechanisms such as their teeth, musky odor (that is comparable to a skunk), small size, speed, and intelligence Foxes are not dangerous to humans except when they are rabid (which is very rare) or if they feel like they are in danger. Foxes are more likely to flee rather than fight

Animal Habitat Information

Foxes can be found living in many different areas like forests, grasslands, mountains and deserts. They make their homes in burrows that they dig.

Animal Anatomy and Biology

Shoulder height of 14”-18” (35-46 cm) Body length between 22”-34” (56-86 cm), An overall weight in the range of 12-18 lb (5-8 kg) Significant facts about foxes They are the only members of the dog family that can climb trees Use the earth’s magnetic field to hunt Arctic foxes don’t shiver until -70 degrees celsius Foxes breathe through their noses and mouths 2-5 years is the average lifespan of foxes
